                      took up cricket at the age of eleven, made his Test match debut on 15 November 1989 against Pakistan in Karachi at the age of sixteen, and went on to represent Mumbai domestically and
India internationally for over 24 years.[11] In 2002, halfway through his career, Wisden ranked him the
second-greatest Test batsman of all time, behind Don Bradman, and the second-greatest ODI batsman of all
time, behind Viv Richards.[12] The same year, Tendulkar was a part of the team that was one of the

team that won the 2011 Cricket World Cup, his first win in six World Cup appearances for India.[13] He
had previously been named "Player of the Tournament" at the 2003 World Cup.
